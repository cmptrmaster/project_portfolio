The Early Years

I started out in college working towards an Electrical Engineering degree at the prestigeous Georgia Institute of Technology. I was accepted to other colleges and universities with excellent engineering schools, but GT was my first choice. During my first year, I entered the Co-Op program and started working for a defense contractor. Although the road to my degree was a little "rocky", I ended up changing my major to Computer Science along the way. Even though I had changed by major, my employer allowed me to stay while finishing up my degree. I ended up staying with them for 20+ years, along with acquiring my Master's degree (MSCIS).

While working for them, I managed their network, servers, PCs, printers, firewalls, databases, phones... if it was infrastructure, my hands were on it. I changed their entire network from ArcNet to Ethernet, moved then from Novell NetWare to Microsoft NT, managed all the hardware and software and everything in between. I worked my way up to the site IT Manager and was in that position for a few years. I managed a small team - a PC technician and a Web developer, but we only had about 200 people at the site. Due to situations outside my control, I had to move on to other things.

Since then...

After leaving the DoD contractor, I started working for the City of Tallahassee, Florida. When I walked in the door in 2011, I had never touched VMware, storage arrays, storage area networks, Citrix or other major infrastructure components. But in the first few years, I became one of their go-to people on those same systems. I turned into the senior engineer on our support team. I lead of team of 4 other engineers and train them on the systems I support so they will also be able to support them and grow in their careers as I did. I work with the network team to architect our datacenter resources for our internal customers and for our public citizens. We maintain a 99.999% uptime through our careful planning and deployment of enterprise class resources.
